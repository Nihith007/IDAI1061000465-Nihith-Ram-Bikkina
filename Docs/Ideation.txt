SECTION 1 — "HOW MIGHT WE" SOLUTIONS IN ACTION
HMW: How might we personalize the experience automatically?

The App Solution: Instead of a complex menu, the app uses a Stress Assessment Hub. As shown in the video, it combines a manual Stress Level Slider (1.0 - 10.0) with a Voice Analysis button and a Wearable Sync toggle. These inputs feed the MoodSync AI to recommend the perfect session.

HMW: How might we help employees decompress in under 15 minutes?

The App Solution: The video showcases a 1-Minute Quick Breathing Reset. By using a visual pulsating circle rather than just audio, the app keeps the user’s visual attention locked, preventing their mind from wandering back to work emails.

HMW: How might we make the post-session feeling visible?

The App Solution: The Post-Session Success Screen. It doesn't just say "Done"; it calculates the points reduced from your stress score and presents a "Badge" (like the 5-Day Streak seen in the video) to provide a hit of dopamine.

SECTION 2 — REFINED COMPULSORY FEATURES
1. MOODSYNC AI (The Intelligent Core)
Visual Representation: Shown as the "Assessment" page in the video.

Functionality: It takes three data points (Slider, Voice, Wearable) and generates a personalized recommendation. In the video, we see it recommend "Forest Refresh" based on the input score of 7.5. It pre-sets the lighting and aroma intensity automatically.

2. BREATHE WITH ME (The Active Intervention)
Visual Representation: The central circular breathing animation.

Functionality: The UI uses text-syncing ("Breathe In," "Hold," "Breathe Out") that expands and contracts. The video shows a Progress Bar at the bottom, which is crucial for busy professionals to know exactly how much time they have left.

3. POD SCHEDULER (The Access)
Visual Representation: The "Quick Actions" sidebar/menu.

Functionality: A simplified booking interface that allows users to reserve a pod instantly. This solves the "stigma" problem by making the break a formal, scheduled event on the calendar.

4. POST-SESSION JOURNAL (The Feedback Loop)
Visual Representation: The "Quick Journal" slider and mood tags.

Functionality: Immediately after the timer ends, the app asks "How do you feel now?" The video shows the user moving the slider to a lower stress number, which the AI then logs to "learn" what works for that specific employee.

5. STRESS TRACKING DASHBOARD (The Data Hub)
Visual Representation: The Home Screen "Stat Cards."

Functionality: Displays four key metrics seen in the video: Sessions Completed, Total Minutes, Current Streak, and Avg. Stress Relief. This appeals to the "Data Analyst" personas who need objective proof of improvement.

SECTION 3 — INNOVATIVE "DELIGHTER" FEATURES (Visible in Video)
Sensory Sliders: The "Pod Controls" screen features dedicated sliders for Aroma Intensity, Lighting, and Soundscape Volume. This allows for manual override if the AI recommendation isn't perfect.

The "Nudge" System: The dashboard includes an AI Insight Card (as seen on the home screen) that suggests the next best time to take a break based on previous high-stress patterns.

Gamification: The Badge System (e.g., the "First Session" and "Streak" badges seen at the end of the video) turns a health chore into a rewarding habit.

SECTION 4 — FINAL PRIORITISATION (Post-Video Iteration)
High Impact (Included): Multi-modal stress assessment, breathing animations, and immediate stress-reduction data.

Future Iteration (V2): Physical hardware integration (opening the actual pod door via the app) and live biometric API syncing for Apple/Garmin watches.
