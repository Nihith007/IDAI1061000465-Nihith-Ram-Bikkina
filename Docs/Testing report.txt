SECTION 1 — TESTING OBJECTIVES
================================================================

  1. Evaluate whether the prototype effectively reduces perceived stress
  2. Identify usability issues across all 9 screens
  3. Assess the quality and relevance of AI recommendations
  4. Measure user satisfaction with the 5 compulsory features
  5. Identify areas for improvement before final submission

================================================================
SECTION 2 — TESTING METHOD
================================================================

FORMAT:
  Each participant was given 20 minutes with the prototype.
  The session included:
    a) Brief orientation (3 min) — told what the app does, not how
    b) Task-based exploration (12 min) — 5 assigned tasks
    c) Rating and feedback (5 min) — survey + verbal debrief

TASKS GIVEN TO EACH PARTICIPANT:
  Task 1: Log in as yourself (or create a new profile)
  Task 2: Complete a stress check and get an AI recommendation
  Task 3: Explore the Pod Setup screen and start a session
  Task 4: Use the Breathe with Me feature for one full round
  Task 5: Complete the post-session journal and view your progress

METRICS COLLECTED:
  - Task completion rate (yes / partial / no)
  - Time on task
  - Usability rating per feature (1–5)
  - Open-ended verbal feedback
  - Overall satisfaction rating (1–5)

SECTION 6 — OBSERVED USABILITY ISSUES (Prioritised)
================================================================

PRIORITY 1 — CRITICAL (Fixed in v2 of prototype):
  ❌ Issue: Breathing guide in Active Session not intuitive
     Observation: T2 and T4 hesitated on the breathing circle
     Fix: Added "Next step" button and clearer phase instruction text

  ❌ Issue: Wearable + Voice labels unclear to non-tech users
     Observation: T3 (Priya) confused by terminology
     Fix: Added descriptive subtitles and icons for each section

PRIORITY 2 — IMPORTANT (Noted for improvement):
  ⚠ Issue: Pod Setup screen has too many controls at once
     Observation: T3 found it overwhelming on first use
     Suggestion: Collapse advanced controls under an "Advanced" toggle

  ⚠ Issue: Timer advance button breaks demo immersion
     Observation: T5 noted this
     Suggestion: Add a "Fast Forward (Demo)" label to clarify intent

  ⚠ Issue: Chart X-axis shows "Session 1, 2" not real dates
     Observation: T4 requested date-based X-axis
     Suggestion: Format X-axis as dates from session history

PRIORITY 3 — NICE TO HAVE (Future version):
  💡 Journal history view — scrollable log of all past entries
  💡 Calendar integration for Pod Scheduler (Google/Outlook)
  💡 Remove "(Simulated)" labels from voice/wearable for cleaner UI
  💡 Weekly summary feature on the Dashboard screen
  💡 Export session data as PDF

SECTION 9 — CONCLUSIONS
================================================================

The Mindful Work prototype was received extremely positively by
all 6 test participants, with an average usability score of 4.4/5
and a 97% task completion rate.

KEY SUCCESSES:
  ✔ All 5 compulsory features were used successfully by all participants
  ✔ MoodSync AI recommendations were perceived as accurate and relevant
  ✔ The private, non-judgmental tone of the app was consistently praised
  ✔ Breathe with Me produced a genuine calming response in 5/6 users
  ✔ The design felt professional and distinct from generic wellness apps

KEY AREAS FOR FUTURE DEVELOPMENT:
  → Real wearable integration (Apple Watch, Fitbit API)
  → Calendar integration for scheduling (Google/Outlook)
  → Data persistence between sessions (database or local storage)
  → Real-time session timer (not click-based)
  → Journal history view with filtering and search

OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
  The prototype successfully demonstrates the core design thinking
  process — from empathy research through to a tested, iterated
  interactive solution. The five compulsory features work together
  as a coherent system, and user feedback confirms that the design
  addresses real pain points identified in the research phase.
